# Q42 — Onboarding Page Draft

*Seen once, during onboarding, before the first check-in. Gives Q42 its own space before the user encounters it in the check-in flow.*

*Last updated: 2026-05-05 — Session 7*

---

**One question we want to talk about before you begin**

There's a question in this check-in that we want to give some space to before you see it for the first time.

It asks whether you're having thoughts that the world would be better without you — or something in that territory. It's question 42, and it's part of every check-in, every time. Not because of anything you've said elsewhere. Just because it matters, and because it deserves to be asked regularly rather than only in a crisis.

---

**What it's asking about**

The question is trying to catch a range of experiences that often go unnamed. Some of them are quiet. Some aren't. You might recognise one of these:

- Feeling like you're a burden to the people around you
- Feeling like you're taking up space you haven't earned
- Thoughts that it would be easier to not exist
- Going through the motions because you can't picture why you'd stay
- A bone-deep exhaustion that isn't physical — more like being tired of being you
- A feeling that people would be sorry — or would finally understand — if you were gone

These experiences don't all look the same. Some feel like sadness. Some feel like exhaustion. Some feel like anger. They belong in the same question because they're all worth knowing about.

---

**What it isn't asking about**

This question is not asking about your beliefs around end-of-life choice, assisted dying, or the right to determine the timing and manner of your own death. Those are legitimate positions that many people — especially people living with chronic illness — hold thoughtfully and seriously. They are not what this question is for, and answering yes to this question does not mean the tool has confused the two.

---

**A few things worth knowing**

*These thoughts are more common than people tend to say out loud.* Most people who have them have never told anyone. If you recognise something on that list, you're not unusual — you're just someone who hasn't had a good place to put it until now.

*Having a thought isn't the same as meaning it.* Fleeting thoughts exist. A thought that passes in seconds, that you don't believe or intend, still counts as a real experience worth tracking. You're not being asked what you're going to do — just what's been present.

*If you've been through this before and you're okay right now* — this question isn't a check on whether you're back there. It's just a question. Answer it for where you are today.

*If you're not sure whether what you're experiencing counts* — it counts. Answer as best you can. The notes field is there if you want to say more.

---

**What happens when you answer**

If you answer anything other than "No," the tool will respond — but not with an alarm, and not by taking over. It will acknowledge what you've said, open a space for you to say more if you want to, and offer some resources in case they're useful. It will not redirect you away from the check-in unless you choose to go.

You stay in control of what happens next.

---

**One last thing**

This question will appear in every check-in. After a while it may feel routine — and that's okay. It's meant to be. A question doesn't have to feel heavy every time to be worth asking.
