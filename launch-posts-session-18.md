### Body

If you've ever filled out a PHQ-9 or GAD-7 at a doctor's office and thought *"these questions aren't built for me,"* this might be for you.

I've been building a mental-health check-in called **No Really. How *Are* You?** It's long-form on purpose — about 45 questions covering sleep, sensory load, masking, shutdowns, social capacity, dissociation, suicidal ideation, and more — because the standard short forms ask 9 questions and miss most of what's actually happening for a lot of neurodivergent people.

It's in testing right now. Every question has a feedback button. There's a wrap-up at the end. The whole thing exists so people can say *"this question is wrong," "this option is missing," "this made me feel weird,"* and the next version gets built around what people actually say.

**A few things to know:**

- **Heavy content.** It covers self-harm, shutdowns, dissociation, masking burnout. The self-harm question (Q42) is currently a placeholder that asks testers what a better version of that question should look like — because the standard wording is one of the things that hurts.
- **Your answers stay on your device.** No account, no cloud, no tracking. The only thing that reaches me is feedback you choose to submit, optionally with a handle if you want.
- **It's long.** The real tool will be shorter — there's a short-form *Low Capacity Mode* planned for bad brain days. This testing version is intentionally maximal so I can see what works and what doesn't.
- **No prescribed way to engage.** Walk through it. Skip questions. Pause. Share whatever you notice however you want.

Not selling anything. No data collection beyond what you explicitly submit. Just genuinely want feedback from people who've been let down by the short forms.

## 3. Creator / Builder Subreddit Post (markdown)

### Title options

- *Built a long-form mental-health check-in for the people PHQ-9 misses. Looking for build feedback.*
- *In-public build: a mental-health check-in for the audience the short forms don't fit*
- *Testing the first tool in a platform I'm calling "External Prefrontal Cortex" — long-form mental health check-in*

### Body

Sharing what I've been building for a few months. Would value eyes from this crowd specifically.

**What it is:** *No Really. How Are You?* — a long-form mental-health check-in for neurodivergent adults who don't see themselves in standard forms like the PHQ-9 and GAD-7. ~45 questions covering sleep, sensory, masking, shutdowns, social capacity, dissociation, suicidal ideation, etc. The eventual platform around it is called *External Prefrontal Cortex* — a set of tools for ND adults to track what's actually going on with them and communicate it to doctors / employers / themselves.

**Where it is:** testing version is live. Single-file HTML/CSS/JS on GitHub Pages. Auto-saves to localStorage. Feedback pipes to a Google Form → Sheet. Supabase migration is next.

**What I'm looking for from this crowd:**

- UX feedback on a long form (45 questions is unusual — does the pacing work? section structure?)
- The two-track build (testing companion file with feedback buttons on every question + clean check-in file) — is the separation clean enough?
- Anything obviously broken on your device
- Honest reactions to the design and tone

**What I'm not looking for:** marketing advice or "have you considered making it shorter." I know it's long. The full check-in is *meant* to be long for a specific audience; there's a short-form mode planned separately.

**Heads up on content:** covers self-harm, dissociation, masking burnout. The self-harm question is currently a placeholder asking testers what a better version should look like.

No business model decided. Not VC-funded. Built with Claude as a coding pair, single-file HTML for now, no framework. Will probably stay free-with-optional-pay if it goes anywhere public.

👉 https://tibbalsgribbin.github.io/external-prefrontal-cortex/no-really-testing.html

Happy to answer questions about the build, the design choices, or anything else.
